The General Guidelines for the Conduct of Random Drug Testing for Secondary and Tertiary Students

1.WHAT IS SUBSTANCE ABUSE?

Substance Abuse is the harmful use or dependence on an addictive substance. These substances can include alcohol and other drugs whether it may be illegal or legal.

2. WHAT IS RANDOM DRUG TESTING?

Random Drug Testing is a procedure to determine the presence or absence of a substance in the body.

3. HOW DOES THE UNIVERSITY SELECT THE STUDENTS?

The selection of student is through an unbiased process of generating random numbers using a computer or a scientific calculato.

4. WHAT SHOULD BE DONE IN CASE THE DRUG TEST IS CONFIRMED?

When a drug result is confirmed positive, the student shall then undergo the prescribed intervention program. This intervention is under the supervision of the Department of Health. The student may also be accompanied by a faculty and parent. Such counselling shall be done in coordination with the Drug Counselor of the school and the Department of Social Welfare and Development.

5. WHAT ARE THE PURPOSES OF RANDOM DRUG TESTING?

a. To determine the prevelance of drug users among the students.

b. To assess the effectivity of school-based and community-based prevention programs.

c. To deter adolescents from initiating the use or illegal drugs.

d. To give adolescents a reason to resist peer pressure to use illegal drugs

e. To facilitate the referral/treatment of drug users and dependents.

f. To strengthen the collaboration efforts of identified agencies against the use of illegal drugs and in the treatment of drug user and dependents
